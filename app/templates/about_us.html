James Wong

James is a transportation planner and engineer, based in New York. He began his career in transportation consulting where he learned where roundabouts are better than traffic signals, why big-box development has mandatory acres of parking around it, and why the old-school public process is stuck in the mud. He did this work mostly around the Mid-Atlantic and even South Africa. After that, he went back to school to nurture his interests in transit and data at the Urban Data Information Lab at Georgia Tech. Almost three years later, he is now in NY where he grew up and is pursuing transportation work in consulting, the public sector, as a free-lancer - wherever the fun work lies.
